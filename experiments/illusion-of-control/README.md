# 🎰 The Illusion of Control

> Langer (1975) sold $1 raffle tickets at Yale. People handed a random ticket asked $1.96 to give it up. People who picked their own number demanded $8.67 — for the same drum, same odds. 8 chance scenarios where the outcome is genuinely random and people behave as if it is not. Picked lottery numbers, dice in your hand, slot button timing, surgeons with identical records. WIZ counts how many times you felt in charge of a coin flip. Strickland Lewicki Katz (1966), Wohl Enzle (2002), Fenton-O'Creevy (2003), Slovic (1987), Taylor (1983).
